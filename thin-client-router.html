Thin-Client → OPNsense Router

Hardware conversion Enclosure design 3D printing
Built · Rev C

A decommissioned 10ZiG 6000q thin client rebuilt into a fanless home router running OPNsense with AdGuard Home. Adding a second network interface broke the stock rear port layout, so the rear I/O plate became its own design problem.

I modeled a replacement rear I/O plate in OpenSCAD and iterated it on the printer — the row of prototypes in the photos is the cutout geometry and mounting tabs being dialed in against caliper-measured dimensions until the ports seated cleanly. Internal storage was solved with an mSATA-to-adapter bodge carrying a KingSpec SSD, and the WAN interface came from an added M.2 A+E NIC alongside the board's onboard Intel controller.

The result is a silent, low-power router that looks intentional rather than hacked together — the enclosure closes flush with a part I designed to the tenth of a millimeter.
